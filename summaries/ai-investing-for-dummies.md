### Distinguishing the Two Types of AI
The author simplifies the broad AI landscape into **two types**: **General (horizontal)** AI and **Specific (vertical)** AI.

**General AI ("horizontal")**
- Spans the vast digital landscape to produce responses to general inquiries or requests.
- Good for starters and general knowledge.
- Example: **ChatGPT** — despite its impressive capabilities, the author wouldn't use it for highly specialized, high-stakes matters (e.g., a web page is fine, but not brain surgery or cancer diagnosis — he'd consult specialized human experts for those).
- Another general-purpose category: **AI-enhanced search engines** (18+ exist at the time of writing; listed in Appendix B).

**Specific AI ("vertical")**
- Concentrates on a given topic or specialty.
- Example: **robo-advisors** (covered in Chapter 7) — technological power applied to a narrow domain.
- Some AI tools specialize in finance, others in healthcare, and so on.
- **Analogy used:** Think of general AI like a general practitioner (GP) doctor — good for an initial visit — while specific AI is like a cardiologist, who you'd be referred to for specialized issues. The GP (general AI) helps you figure out what you need, then directs you to the specialist (specific AI).
- Chapter 3 covers different categories/directories of AI tools to help match the right tool to the right need.

### Investing in Stocks with AI's Help
- The primary reason readers picked up this book: the title combines "AI" and "investing," and the core of that combination is covered in **Part 2** of the book.
- Important distinction: **investing vs. speculating**.
  - *Investing* = putting money into assets/vehicles that take time to grow in value (e.g., large-cap stocks) — "educated gambling," hopefully with greater certainty over time.
  - *Speculating* = chasing quick, dramatic gains (e.g., small-cap or "ka-ching" tech stocks) — closer to true gambling; greater risk, greater (and faster) potential reward or loss.
  - Readers should know the difference between the two before deciding where AI fits into their strategy.
- For **large-cap stocks** (stable, established companies), see **Chapter 6** for fundamentals-based, longer-term analysis ("fundamentals" = the heart of true investing).
- For **small-cap/speculative stocks**, see **Chapter 8**.
- For a **safer approach to AI-related investing**, consider **ETFs (exchange-traded funds)** and **mutual funds** that invest in AI — covered in **Chapter 6**.

- Investing is only one (large) part of the overall financial planning process; **Part 4** of the book addresses budgeting and other financial-planning topics.
- AI can't fully replace a human expert for tougher matters like estate planning and taxes, but it makes the process of dealing with these areas easier, reduces stress, and increases knowledge/confidence.

### Key Definitions (Chapter 1)
- **AI (Artificial Intelligence):** A computer system that can understand information, make decisions, and solve problems without being explicitly programmed for every task, using data and patterns to learn and predict.
- **Prompt:** A question or request typed into an AI chatbot to generate a response.
- **General/Horizontal AI:** Broad-purpose AI usable across many types of inquiries (e.g., ChatGPT, AI search engines).
- **Specific/Vertical AI:** AI focused on a single domain or specialty (e.g., robo-advisors for investing).
- **Investing vs. Speculating:** Investing = longer-term, more measured asset growth; Speculating = short-term, high-risk/high-reward bets.

### Key Takeaways (Chapter 1)
- AI is now a major force across business, social, and financial life — exciting but should be approached thoughtfully.
- AI splits broadly into general (horizontal) tools for wide-ranging questions and specific (vertical) tools for specialized domains — use the right tool for the right job, just like choosing between a GP and a specialist doctor.
- The book's primary focus is using AI to assist with stock investing (Part 2), but AI also extends to alternative assets (Part 3: metals, crypto, real estate), business/career (Chapter 12), and full financial planning (Part 4), including macro analysis of the economy (Chapter 13).
- Know the difference between investing and speculating before applying AI to your strategy.
- AI is evolving quickly; rely on continually updated resources (the book's appendices, AI directories, and educational sites) to stay current.

## Chapter 2: A Primer on ChatGPT

### Overview
This chapter introduces ChatGPT specifically — its origin, versions, capabilities, limitations, and alternatives — and walks through example prompts geared toward financial planning and investing.

### Acquainting Yourself with ChatGPT
- AI hit the public's radar dramatically in 2022 with the launch of ChatGPT, much faster and more visible than previous consumer-AI moments (e.g., annoying voices behind Alexa/Siri).
- ChatGPT was created and launched by **OpenAI**, accessible at **chat.openai.com**.
- Two versions existed at the time of writing:
  - **Free version**: GPT version 3.5.
  - **Premium version**: **ChatGPT Plus**, using GPT version 4, costing **$20/month**, offering more power and potential.
- ChatGPT hit **one million users in record time** — much faster than prior tech launches like Netflix (took years to reach comparable milestones).
- OpenAI offers extensive help resources, listed in Appendix A; a good starting point is https://help.openai.com/en/.

### Asking ChatGPT to Describe Itself
- The author demonstrates a simple prompt: "What is ChatGPT?"
- **Figure 2-1** (described): Screenshot of ChatGPT's self-description response, explaining that it's a language model developed by OpenAI, part of the GPT (Generative Pre-trained Transformer) family, specifically GPT-3.5; trained on diverse internet text; designed to understand and generate human-like text; known for generating coherent, contextually relevant responses in conversational interactions; can take input as prompts or queries; useful for a wide range of NLP (natural language processing) applications including content creation, text completion, language translation, and more. ### Creating Your Own Prompts: A Simple Formula
The author gives a **two-step formula** for creating a useful prompt (until you get proficient):
1. **Give the AI tool a scenario** as the first part of your prompt.
   - Example: "I am a 60-year-old single woman with $X amount in my 401(k) plan..."
2. **Then give a specific question** as the second part of your prompt.
   - Example: "...and I am considering a growth fund, but I want to compare the pros and cons with a growth-and-income fund."
- Once proficient, explore ChatGPT's expanding uses and add-ons at https://platform.openai.com/examples.

### The Limits and Negatives of ChatGPT
ChatGPT is far from perfect. Key drawbacks/glitches to keep in mind:
- **A limit on knowledge:** ChatGPT 3.5 (free version) has a knowledge cutoff (January 2022 at the time of writing) — it doesn't have real-time data.
- **Bias:** Questions about philosophy, politics, public policy, and related areas where human bias is strong can produce skewed answers. The author notes the "garbage in equals garbage out" saying applies, since humans are involved in both programming AI and supplying its training data.
- **Incorrect answers:** Bugs and glitches mean there's a possibility of errors and poor/inaccurate responses — multiple sources should be checked, especially for sensitive or important topics.
- **Cannot provide personal advice:** AI tools generally don't have access to personal data, so avoid asking personal questions; you can give it scenarios/example problems, but it won't take a mindful, sentient-guidance role like a human expert.
- **Other issues with AI:** The author won't try to be comprehensive about all of AI's failings and shortcomings here — it isn't an emotional being and won't take personal criticism, but he encourages awareness of its limitations.

### Key Definitions (Chapter 2)
- **ChatGPT:** A conversational AI chatbot created by OpenAI, based on the GPT (Generative Pre-trained Transformer) language model family.
- **GPT (Generative Pre-trained Transformer):** The underlying language model architecture/family that powers ChatGPT (versions referenced: GPT-3.5 free, GPT-4 in ChatGPT Plus).
- **Prompt:** The user's input — a question, request, or scenario — that generates the AI's response.

## Chapter 3: Spanning the AI Landscape: Different Inputs and Outputs

### Overview
This chapter broadens the lens beyond text-based chatbots to the full range of AI "input-to-output" modes — text, audio, image, video, and more — explaining how each format can serve investing, business, and personal financial planning needs. The chapter frames AI as a secret weapon for investment pursuits, noting that hundreds of AI tools and apps now exist (likely thousands by the time readers encounter this book), so only the most durable, useful tools are highlighted.

### First Things First: Text-to-Text with Chatbots
- ChatGPT (covered in depth in Chapter 2) is an AI designed to interact with users via questions and requests (prompts), returning answers in text format — the most common and familiar AI interaction.
- ChatGPT is described as fluent in languages — for example, you can paste a 500-word Spanish paragraph and have it translated into English (or another language) in seconds.
- The chatbot "role-plays": you can ask for an answer "as if you were a fifth grader," or "as if it were told by Darth Vader, Tarzan, or Donald Duck," demonstrating its flexible, patient, all-knowing-robot character.
- Examples of text-to-text utility:
  - **Language power:** Translate text fluently between languages instantly.
  - **Learn a new skill:** In a competitive economy, the more skills you have, the more employable/valuable you become; AI can teach skills inexpensively from home rather than paying for expensive courses or tutors.
  - **Do programming for you:** Design a webpage in HTML or Python, create Excel formulas your boss needs, all without needing deep technical knowledge — ask a detailed question and get a step-by-step answer in seconds.
  - **What do you imagine you need?** Examples given: a poem for a 30th wedding anniversary (the author's own example — his wife was impressed), a resume in seconds, a business plan, a marketing checklist. "The uses are endless."
- The author reiterates that AI is a perfect tool for investors — e.g., building a custom spreadsheet to analyze a public company's finances or do retirement projections — and that the rest of the book's text-to-text focus will provide many applicable examples.

### Other AI Inputs and Outputs
Beyond text-to-text, several other AI formats can benefit professional, career, and investing pursuits:

**Document-to-text**
- One of the most useful AI functions in today's information-overloaded age — extracting usable, succinct meaning from voluminous documentation.
- Example tool: **ChatPDF** (www.chatpdf.com) — analyzes or summarizes a lengthy PDF.
- Useful for investors/speculators: reviewing and summarizing online documents such as research reports, investment posts, and other detailed documents.

**Audio-to-text**
- The author does videos on economics, investing, business, and related topics, and uses AI tools to transcribe spoken words into text (for training materials or e-books for clients/students).
- Process: record a lecture (via digital voice recorder or smartphone) as a WAV or MP3 file, then upload it to a transcription service such as **Otter** (www.otter.ai) to get a completed transcript in minutes.
- For investors, audio-to-text AI can turn investing classes and lectures into transcripts for easier review/reference.

**Text-to-image**
- Previously required hiring a graphic designer and waiting hours; now you provide a text description of the desired image and the AI generates it for free or low cost.
- Top text-to-image AI tools listed:
  - **Midjourney** (www.midjourney.com)
  - **DALL-E 3** (https://openai.com/dall-e-3)
  - **Leonardo.Ai** (www.leonardo.ai)
- Use cases:
  - **Business uses:** Creating images for a website or any business need — describe what you want (the text input) and AI produces many image variations. Example given: describing an "ice cream cone" image (chocolate and strawberry, rainbow sprinkles in a waffle cone) and having it generated in seconds.
  - **Financial uses:** Describe how well a given stock, asset, or investment vehicle has performed against a benchmark (e.g., the Dow or S&P 500) to the AI tool, and it can create a chart so you can judge performance visually.

**Text-to-video**
- For personal or business needs, AI can create video from text descriptions (such as an article or post).
- Top tools for this task listed:
  - **Pictory.ai** (www.pictory.ai)
  - **Hour One** (www.hourone.ai)
  - **Flexclip** (www.flexclip.com)

**Image-to-insight / data visualization note**
- A simple picture can be more powerful and useful than a mountain of dry data.
- Example: **Visual Capitalist** (www.visualcapitalist.com) — excellent at sifting, analyzing, and presenting data, taking it one level beyond a simple visual, useful for investment decision-making.
- Anecdote: an analyst noticed that movement in junk bonds was an early precursor to subsequent movement in stocks (the S&P 500) — when junk bonds rose, the S&P 500 followed; when junk bonds declined, the S&P 500 declined too. He found this in a chart (a visual), which was the culmination of extensive data analysis.
- **Tip:** Using AI tools mentioned in the chapter (and Appendix B), readers can have AI extract data for two or more interrelated markets/vehicles to see whether and how closely they correlate (e.g., how closely are bond markets and stock markets correlated across economic cycles? How closely is gold related to the inflation rate?). This is presented as a way AI can serve as an alert system for investing and trading strategies.
